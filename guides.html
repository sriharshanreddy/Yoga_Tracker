Beginner's Guide
30-45 minutes

Getting Started with Yoga

Preparation

  • Find a quiet, comfortable space with enough room to move
  • Use a yoga mat or non-slip surface
  • Wear comfortable clothing that allows movement
  • Have a water bottle nearby
  • Practice on an empty stomach (2-3 hours after eating)

Basic Yoga Sequence

1. Mountain Pose (Tadasana)

30 seconds

Steps: Stand tall with feet together, arms at sides. Distribute weight evenly. Engage thighs, lift kneecaps. Lengthen tailbone toward floor. Broaden collarbones. Breathe deeply.

Benefits: Improves posture, strengthens thighs and core, increases awareness.

2. Cat-Cow Stretch

1 minute

Steps: Start on hands and knees (tabletop position). Inhale, drop belly, lift chin and chest (Cow). Exhale, round spine, tuck chin to chest (Cat). Repeat 5-8 times.

Benefits: Warms up spine, improves flexibility, massages organs.

3. Downward-Facing Dog

30 seconds - 1 minute

Steps: From tabletop, tuck toes, lift hips up and back. Straighten legs (microbend in knees if needed). Press hands firmly, ears between arms. Heels toward floor.

Benefits: Strengthens arms and legs, stretches hamstrings, calms mind.

4. Child's Pose

1-2 minutes

Steps: Kneel on mat, big toes touching, knees wide. Sit back onto heels, fold forward. Extend arms or rest them alongside body. Forehead to mat.

Benefits: Relaxes spine, hips and thighs; relieves stress and fatigue.

Tip: Focus on your breath throughout the practice. Inhale and exhale through the nose, making the breath slightly louder than normal to help maintain focus.

Morning Routine
20-30 minutes

Energizing Morning Yoga

Sequence Overview

  1. Sun Salutations (Surya Namaskar) - 3 rounds

    2. Stand tall, inhale arms up. Exhale fold forward. Inhale half lift. Exhale plank to chaturanga. Inhale upward dog. Exhale downward dog. Step or jump forward. Repeat.

  2. Warrior I (Virabhadrasana I) - 30 seconds each side

    3. From downward dog, step right foot forward. Square hips forward, arms reach up. Keep back leg strong.

  3. Tree Pose (Vrksasana) - 30 seconds each side

    4. Stand on one leg, place other foot on inner thigh or calf (not knee). Hands at heart or overhead.

  4. Bridge Pose (Setu Bandhasana) - 1 minute

    5. Lie on back, knees bent. Lift hips up, interlace fingers under back. Keep thighs parallel.

  5. Seated Forward Bend (Paschimottanasana) - 1-2 minutes

    6. Sit with legs straight. Hinge at hips to fold forward. Use strap if needed.

Best Time to Practice

Ideal between 5-7 AM on empty stomach. Can be done anytime in morning to energize your day.

Benefits
  • Boosts energy and circulation
  • Improves focus and mental clarity
  • Strengthens and stretches entire body
